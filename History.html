history of Svaneti

Svaneti is a historic region located in the northwestern part of Georgia, in the Caucasus Mountains. Known for its rugged terrain and unique cultural heritage, Svaneti has a rich history that has shaped its distinctive character. Here is a brief overview:

Ancient and Medieval Periodsh

Early Inhabitants: The region has been inhabited since ancient times. The Svans, the indigenous people of Svaneti, have lived in the area for centuries, maintaining a distinct language (Svan) and culture.

Classical Antiquity: Svaneti was part of the ancient kingdom of Colchis, renowned in Greek mythology for the story of Jason and the Argonauts. The region later became part of the Kingdom of Lazica in the early Middle Ages.

Christianization: Svaneti converted to Christianity in the early medieval period, around the 6th century. Numerous medieval churches and monasteries were built, many of which contain beautiful frescoes and icons.

Medieval Fortifications Defensive Towers: One of the most notable features of Svaneti is its medieval stone towers, built between the 9th and 13th centuries. These towers served as protection against invaders and natural disasters. Villages like Mestia and Ushguli are famous for these structures, which are now UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Feudal Period

Independent Duchy: During the medieval period, Svaneti functioned as a semi-independent duchy within the Kingdom of Georgia. The region was relatively isolated, which helped preserve its unique culture and traditions.

Mongol Invasions: Svaneti experienced invasions by the Mongols in the 13th century, although its remote location provided some protection.

Early Modern Period

Ottoman and Persian Influence: In the 16th and 17th centuries, Svaneti faced pressure from the expanding Ottoman and Persian empires, but its mountainous terrain continued to offer a degree of insulation from direct control.

Division and Conflict: The region was

divided into two parts:Lower Svaneti and Upper Svaneti.

Internal conflicts and feuds were common among local chieftains.

19th and 20th Centuries Russian Empire: In the 19th century, Svaneti was incorporated into the Russian Empire. This period saw increased Russian influence and the introduction of new administrative practices.

Soviet Era: During the Soviet period, Svaneti underwent significant changes, including the collectivization of agriculture and the development of infrastructure. However, its remote location allowed it to retain much of its traditional way of life.

Modern Era

Post-Soviet Period: After Georgia gained independence in 1991, Svaneti experienced economic difficulties and a decline in population. However, the region has seen a revival in recent years, driven by tourism and efforts to preserve its cultural heritage.

Tourism and Preservation: Today, Svaneti is a popular destination for tourists, attracted by its stunning landscapes, historic architecture, and vibrant culture. The preservation of its medieval towers and churches remains a priority for both the Georgian government and international organizations.

Svaneti's unique combination of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural richness makes it one of Georgia's most fascinating regions.

Lower Svaneti Sights

Main town Lentexi

Skaldi

The Archangels church of Skladi is located on Skaldi mountain at 2210 meters above sea level, in the northwest of the village of Paki. According to locals, the Skaldi church was built in the 10th-11th centuries, although there is very little left of its initial appearance. Monastic life in Skaldi was renewed in 2013. In the church, you will find the icons of Michael and Gabriel Archangels and the icon of the Savior. They are the main relics of the Skladi church.

Resort Muashi

The resort is distinguished for its marvelous nature. Wide fields, woods, picturesque views from the mountain, and woody cottages create a perfect combination for those, who love resting in nature. Muashi is a balneological resort. Damp climate is characteristic of it. The main spa and mineral factor is carbon dioxide, lithium bicarbonate, calcium-sodium mineral water, which is used for both: drinking and bathing. If you want to rest in the calmness of nature and get health treatment as well, Muashi is a wonderful place for it.

Mineral water in Lentexi

Lentekhi mineral water spring is a popular natural attraction located in the Svaneti region, Georgia. This spring is known for its healing properties, which attract both locals and tourists seeking to improve their health. Mineral water from this source is rich in beneficial microelements and is widely used for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

Adventures

Koruldi Lakes

Kolurdli lakes are located on the south slope of the caucuses mountains to the north from Mestia at 2750 MSL. From the lakes wonderful views on Svaneti, mountain Tetnuldi and caucuses mountains are revealed.

Monastery of St. Kvirike and St. Ivlita of Kala

Monastery of St. Kvirike and St. Ivlita of Kala is located south of the Chkhutneri Range in Mestia Municipality. Its height varies from 1870 meters above sea level. It is called Lagurka in Svan. It’s is built in the X century. The church is of the hall type. In the Middle Ages it was a monastic complex.

Lamaria

is a church of the Mother of God, located on an elevated hill in Svaneti, in the Ushguli community of Mestia Municipality, north of the village Zhibiani. The church has a fence around it with a fortified tower. It is a basilica-type building of the IX-X centuries, with a three-sided, projecting apse.
